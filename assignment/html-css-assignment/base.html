Tourist sites in the Western Region of Ghana

Ghana’s Western Region is indeed a treasure trove of culture, history, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for both lone travelers and history enthusiasts. For a better understanding of the area, you can view a map of the Western Region here. Here are some of the top attractions you should consider adding to your bucket list when visiting this remarkable part of Ghana:

Historic Sites

  • Fort Metal Cross

    Located in Dixcove, this fort was originally built by the British in the 17th century. It's a poignant reminder of the region's colonial past and offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean ... Read more

  • Fort San Sebastian

    In an area known as Shama, on the coast of Ghana’s Western Region, lies Ghana’s third oldest fortification. Fort San Sebastian is a historical architectural delight, reflecting the distinctive styles and preferences of both its Portuguese and Dutch sculpturing. Fort San Sebastian was built by the Portuguese from 1520 to 1526. Its original purpose was to serve as a deterrent to English sailors interfering in Shama trade ... Read more

  • Nzulezu Stilt Village

    A UNESCO World Heritage site, this village is built entirely on stilts over Lake Tadane. It offers a unique glimpse into the traditional lifestyles of the local Nzema people ... Read more
