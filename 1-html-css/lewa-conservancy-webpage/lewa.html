Lewa Wildlife Conservancy

Overview and Brief History

Lewa conservancy also known as Lewa Downs is approximately 259.5 kilometres from Nairobi, located in Meru County, south of Isiolo town. The conservancy was founded by the Craig family and Anna Merz. The Craig family used the land as a cattle ranch for 50 years when they arrived in 1920s. Their ranch was different as they valued the local wildlife and they shared it with wild animals even allowing tourists to visit.

During the 1980s period Anna Merz and Craig decided to build a house to protect the Rhinos which were critically threatened by poaching. They started with just 5,000 acres of land in which they fenced with electric fence and their effort was such a great success that they had to relocate many of the Rhinos in Northern Kenya to protect them. Their conservation efforts were so successful that tourist across the world started visiting. These made the Craig family to devote the entire 40,000 acre ranch to create the Lewa conservancy. The ranch is fenced, with just a few gaps to enable the movements of animals in and out of the conservancy into other wildlife protected areas around the conservancy.

Lewa Safari Marathon

This is an annual fund raising event that has been held at the conservancy since 2000. The Lewa Marathon has become widely known for its difficult terrain and harsh climate, drawing competitors and spectators from all over the world. Each year participants share the reserve with several large African predators including the big five. The year 2024 is no different and the event will be held on this beautiful African reserve. For more info you can visit the Lewa Safari Marathon website.

Wildlife Profile of The Conservancy

The Conservancy is home to a wide variety of wildlife including the rare and endangered black rhinos, Grevy’s zebras. The swamp has become a sanctuary in itself for the rare and fascinating semi-aquatic Sitatunga Antelope and its primary predator, the Leopard. In the nighttime, leopards are frequently encountered along with bush-babies, aardvarks, bat-eared foxes, caracal and various mongooses, genets and civets.
